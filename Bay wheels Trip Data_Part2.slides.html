Effects of user type on bike usage¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at how the bike usage in Bay Area is affected by the type of users, whether they were customers or subscribers.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted of duration of travel, start time, end time, start station, end station, start latitude and longitude, end latitude and longitude and some other characteristics like user tpe, rental access and bike share for all. I computed the distance using the haversine formula and added it to the dataset, to get a sense of the distance travelled. The dataset is for the month of November 2019.

Distribution of duration¶

The distribution is unimodal with a long tail. The variable has most of its values around 1000. The maximum value is around 85000.

Distance vs time¶

I assumed distance vs time to have a positive correlation and expected it to have a linear relationship. But, it had a relationship where distance peaked and fell after that. I assume it was because the person used to reach a destination and spend time there, eventually returning to their initial start point.

Impact of user type on station usage based on mean duration of trip¶

We were trying to see whether there is any overlap on station usage based on user type. We split the data into three types- one for distance < 1 mi, another between 1 and 2 mi and the third greater than 2 mi. I didn't see any overlap in the usage of stations furthering the divide between the two user types.
